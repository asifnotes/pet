Cat Care Guidance

Everything you need to keep your feline friend happy and healthy.

Nutrition

  • Balanced Diet: Cats are obligate carnivores and need a meat-based diet rich in protein, taurine, and essential amino acids.
  • Feeding Schedule: Feed adult cats twice daily at consistent times.
  • Hydration: Provide fresh, clean water at all times.
  • Treats: Limit treats to no more than 5% of daily caloric intake.

Hygiene & Grooming

  • Regular Grooming: Brush or comb daily to remove loose fur and reduce hairballs.
  • Clean Litter Box: Scoop daily; fully change litter every 2‚Äì3 weeks.
  • Clean Bowls: Wash food and water dishes daily.
  • Baths: Only as needed; use cat-specific shampoo and dry thoroughly.

Environment

  • Stimulating Space: Provide vertical perches, scratching posts, and a variety of toys.
  • Playtime: Engage in daily interactive sessions for exercise and mental stimulation.
  • Quiet Area: Offer a peaceful spot for rest and relaxation.
  • Aging Adjustments: Add ramps or low steps as your cat gets older.

Health & Healthcare

  • Veterinary Care: Establish routine checkups, vaccinations, and dental cleanings.
  • Parasite Control: Use monthly flea, tick, and worm preventatives as recommended.
  • Spaying/Neutering: Reduces health risks and unwanted behaviors.
  • Pet Insurance: Consider a policy to help cover unexpected vet bills.
  • Early Detection: Monitor behavior, appetite, and litter habits; consult your vet on any changes.

Important Considerations

  • Safety: Remove toxic plants and secure household chemicals.
  • Exercise: Encourage movement through toys and climbing structures.
  • Mental Enrichment: Use puzzle feeders, hide treats, and rotate toys.
  • Social Interaction: Spend quality time; supervise multi-cat introductions.
  • Adoption: Be prepared to adjust your lifestyle and home for a new feline family member.
